My Failures

This page is inspired by Courtney Brousseau . I never knew him personally, but the type of person he seemed to be and the effect he had on the people around him was abundantly apparent. On his website he talked about the importance of acknowledging ones own failures and I hope that my attempt at this does some justice to his memory.

The Berkeley Forum

I joined The Berkeley Forum my freshmen year of college. It was one of two clubs I had joined upon coming to Cal, and the people in the club were some of the most motivated, aware and intelligent people on campus. I helped the club with technology at events, setting up audio equipment and managing most of the our general technology equipment.

However, my failure came at my leadership in the Technology Committee, where I did not progress the committee as I had hoped to do. I had wanted for members to learn more and hold workshops and begin working on projects that could help the club as a whole, but ultimately had nothing to show for my time over the semester. I'm glad to see the technology committee has since recruited some amazing new members who have done some great projects, and helped contrbute to the great club that The Berkeley Forum is

Bomotix

At the end of freshmen year, I had begun taking an interest in machine learning, specifically with more advanced techniques and stacks, due mostly to my time interning with CyberCube. I had first hand seen a lot of the cool work that could be done with Spark and distrubted computing and AWS's machine learning frameworks. One of my friend's dads had told me in a brief encounter that he was woring on developing machine learning models for realtime analysis of body movements for sports using videos that coaches or players recording, an idea that combined my passion for videography, machine learning and sports

All in all, this unpaid opportunity was the perfect fit for me. I talked with the founder of the product (the friend's dad) and had even begun to do some work with it. Unfortunately, I ran out of time during my sophomore year, and began to neglect the responsibilities and despite trying on and off for more than a half a year I hadn't done as much as I had hoped, or I was expected to. I regret that I squandered the opportunity to this extent. It was a good learning opportunity for me not to overcommit, a tendency I have that arises from overconfidence, and I will keep this in mind for future opportunites. The company, Bomotix, is doing amazing work and I highly recommend checking them out and I hope that someday in the future when I have more time I can return to the company and help them develop their vision even more.
